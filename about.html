What is Global Warming anyway?

Global warming is the rapid increase in Earth’s average surface temperature. This change is long term and lasts for a very long time- it’s not something that can disappear with a snap of your fingers. Climate change is a real issue, “satellites have helped scientists collect information about our planet and its climate all over the world to reveal patterns of change,” (Climate Change). Global warming causes an increase in wildfires, rising sea-levels, and other natural events like droughts (“Overview of Greenhouse Gases).

Source: (Lindsey)

Ok, I know what it is, but who causes it?

Global warming is largely driven by human activities like the burning of fossil fuels which releases greenhouse gases that trap heat in the atmosphere (Clark and Lee 592). Not only that, powering homes and businesses produces massive amounts of CO2 which is also bad for the environment. This process adds to rising global temperatures, which as I said above, is no good.

Can we really even do anything?

Eh, kind of. While not eating meat or riding the bike won't save the world, these collective actions can really make an impact. Ways we can can switch to renewable sources of energy (such as solar and wind energy) to power our homes and buildings, can drive electric vehicles instead of those that burn fossil fuels, we can support more local businesses that use and promote sustainable, climate-smart practices, and you can even think of more (Herring 1)! So if only you decide to make a change, the world isn't changing. If we all work to change- then of course!.
