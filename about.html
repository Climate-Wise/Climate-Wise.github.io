What is Global Warming anyway?

Global warming is the rapid increase in Earth’s average surface temperature. This change is long term and lasts for a very long time- it’s not something that can disappear with a snap of your fingers. Climate change is a real issue, “satellites have helped scientists collect information about our planet and its climate all over the world to reveal patterns of change,” (Climate Change). Global warming causes an increase in wildfires, rising sea-levels, and other natural events like droughts. (Climate Change).

Ok, I know what it is, but who causes it?

While large industries and energy companies are the biggest contributors, our collective daily choices—the cars we drive, the energy we use at home, and the products we buy—all play a part. We are all connected to the cycle.

Can we really even do anything?

Yes! Individual action creates a "ripple effect." When you lower your footprint, you influence your friends, family, and companies to change. Large-scale change always starts with informed individuals taking the first step.
